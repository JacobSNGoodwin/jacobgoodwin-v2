## Source Scriptures

- [Acts 1-5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/1?lang=eng)

## Summary

- Acts is a second book written by Luke to Theophilus [Bible Dictionary](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bd/acts-of-the-apostles?lang=eng)
- Acts 1-6 have a heavy focus on Peter. Later chapters shift focus to Paul.
- Replacement for Judas is selected
- Disciples receive Holy Ghost on the Day of Pentecost
- Manifestation of the Gift of Tongues
  - Peters testifies of Christ
  - He teaches how people can follow Christ
- Peter and John heal a man
  - Peter testifies of Christ
  - He teaches how people can follow Christ
- Peter and John taken into custody by priests and Sadducees
  - Peter testifies of Christ
- Grace is upon followers as they pray and testify of Jesus
- Disciples consecrate their goods and have all things in common
- Angel of the Lord releases (unnamed) Apostles from prison
  - Apostles testify in temple and after being presented before the council

## Importance of the Holy Ghost

<InfoContainer title="To Ponder">

What do you learn from the experiences of these early Apostles about how you can rely on the Holy Ghost to guide you? How can we seek the Holy Ghost's inspiration?

How is meeting together as disciples important?

</InfoContainer>

- While Jesus dwells with disciples for 40 days after his resurrection, he promises the Holy Ghost will give them power to be witnesses to all the world. [Acts 1:3-8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/1.3-8?lang=eng#p3)
- Apostles, "the women", and Mary (mother of Jesus) pray in on accord. [Acts 1:13-15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/1.13-15?lang=eng#13)
  - New Apostle chosen "to be a witness with us of His resurrection." [Acts 1:22-26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/1.22-26?lang=eng#p22)
- Disciples are again together in one accord on day of Pentecost (Shavuoth) as Holy Ghost is poured out on people. [Acts 2:1-4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/2.1-4?lang=eng#p1)
  - I find noteworthy the fact that the disciples come together on the last two occasions.
- Holy Ghost causes speaking in tongues. [Acts 2:5-13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/2.5-13?lang=eng#p5)
  - People of different languages in Jerusalem for festival understand in their own language
- Disciples accused of being drunk when speaking in tongues. Peter uses opportunity to testify of Christ - [Acts 2:14-47](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/2.14-17?lang=eng#p14)
  - In last days, Christ will pour out his spirit upon all - v.17
  - Peter's witness of Christ's resurrection - v.22-24
  - What we do when our hearts are pricked - v.37-47

## Boldly Testifying of Christ

<InfoContainer title="To Ponder">

What are some situations you face where you may feel pressured to not live or speak the way you believe? How do you handle these situations even if you feel your testimony is lacking?

</InfoContainer>

- Peter and John heal lame man on way to temple. [Acts 3:1-11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/3.1-11?lang=eng#p1)
  - When people marvel, Peter testifies of Jesus Christ. [Acts 3:12-26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/3.12-26?lang=eng#p12)
  - v.19 - Repent!
- Peter and John, taken in by rulers, are threatened to not speak anymore - Acts 4
  - Peter testifies healing is through Christ - [Acts 4:8-12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/4.8-12?lang=eng#p8)
  - Rulers recognize that the miracle is undeniable, all they can do is threaten them to not speak
    - "For we cannot but speak the things which we have seen and heard." [Acts 4:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/4.20?lang=eng#p20)
  - No charges are brought against Peter and John, who are released. Group of saints again come together to pray and give thanks - v.23-37
- Sapphira and Ananias lie about their consecration and are struck dead- [Acts 5:1-11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/5.1-11?lang=eng#p1)
- As more people witness undeniable miracles and join the saints, Apostles are again taken into custody - [Acts 5:12-18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/5.12-18?lang=eng#p12)
  - Angel frees them and commands them to preach. They preach in the temple, but are again brought peacefully to the council (because council fears the people who are more and more believing in Christ) - v.19-28
  - "We ought to obey God rather than men." [Acts 5:29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/5.29?lang=eng#p29)
    - Peter continues to testify of the resurrection of Christ - v.30-33
- Gamaliel's explanation of why they shouldn't make trouble with apostles - [Acts 5:34-40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/5.34-40?lang=eng#p34)
  - If the work is of men, it will come to not. If it is of God, you cannot overthrow it. -v.38-39
- Apostles are beaten and sent off, happy have suffered for Christ's name. They continue in the temple daily.
